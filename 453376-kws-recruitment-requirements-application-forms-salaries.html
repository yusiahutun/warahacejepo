KWS recruitment requirements, application forms, and salaries

October 05, 2024
blog

Applying for the Kenya Wildlife Service(KWS) and getting a position can be challenging. However, if you have the correct information, you are more likely to be shortlisted and get the job. First, you must familiarise yourself with the KWS recruitment requirements for the job you are applying for. Then, understand the application process.

It is a dream of most Kenyans to land parastatal jobs. Most of these jobs are permanent and pensionable, and their offer a myriad of allowances. Kenya Wildlife Service carries out the recruitment exercise across the country. Prospective applicants must meet KWS recruitment requirements to work for the organisation. So, what are the requirements?

Kenya Wildlife Society jobs

The Kenya Wildlife Service focuses on the conservation and management of wildlife. This wildlife has promoted various things in the country, such as job creation and tourist attractions. As a result, the government earns more revenue, and the employees make a living.

KWS job announcements are made public through their website. The organisation discourages any form of canvassing because it will lead to automatic disqualification. Additionally, the recruitment process is open to qualified candidates.

KWS recruitment requirements

The recruitment requirements vary depending on the job description. These requirements will be listed alongside the job whenever there is a vacancy. However, there are mandatory requirements for eligibility.

These are what are considered for eligibility:

  • Be a Kenyan citizen based on birth or descent.
  • Certified by government medical personnel to be physically and mentally suitable for appointment in services.
  • Must hold at least a KCSE certificate.
  • Someone with a good character (is not guilty of criminal offences).

KWS recruitment portal

To ensure that the recruitment process is efficient and corruption-free, most organisations have developed an online recruitment platform. But unfortunately, the Kenya Wildlife Society does not have a recruitment portal. Instead, all job announcements are made via their website with the KWS recruitment application form attached.

What to avoid while applying for the KWS jobs

Many people miss out not because they are not qualified but because they make mistakes during the application. So, here are what you need to take note of:

  • Apply on time and do not wait for the deadlines.
  • Ensure that you do the application process alone and not send someone to do it.
  • Keep yourself updated concerning the application process.
  • Candidates should only apply for one job position.
  • Ensure you send all the requirements.

KWS rangers recruitment is done across the country. The exercise is similar to recruiting police officers or the Kenyan army. In case of any vacancy, the recruitment centres are announced. Short-listed applicants participate in a physical exercise as part of the selection process.

KWS warns candidates of unscrupulous people who may pose as recruitment officers while the exercise is ongoing.

Can you join KWS with a D?

While recruiting rangers, the KWS seeks to get men and women who can brave the harsh, remote and hostile climatic conditions. These are the conditions that rangers work in. Additionally, other requirements are that candidates must have a maximum grade of D+ in the KCSE. Therefore, if you scored a D in KCSE, it is possible to join and work for the organisation.

How much do KWS rangers earn?

KWS officers' salaries vary drastically depending on many factors. These factors could include KWS rangers' ranks, work experience, gender, location, and level of education. The recruited rangers earn a salary range of Ksh.25,000 to Ksh. 50,000 per month plus allowances.

Newly recruited graduates in KWS earn a salary range of Ksh. 50,000 to Ksh. 75,000 per month with allowances.

KWS internship positions

The Kenya Wildlife Service offers internship opportunities to qualified applicants. The program equips participants with real-life experience besides the theoretical teachings in the classroom. They seek to train applicants in the following areas:

  • Wildlife Management and Environmental science
  • Statistics
  • ICT
  • Information Science and Library
  • Procurement
  • Finance/ Accounting
  • Human Resource Management
  • Communication and Public Relations

The organisation has a set of internship requirements that you need before being offered the opportunity. The requirements are as follows:

  • A letter of application and updated curriculum vitae.
  • You should have completed your training and graduated in the previous year.
  • Be a holder of at least a diploma from a recognised institution in your preferred area of specialisation.
  • A recommendation letter from your learning institution.
  • An applicant must be a Kenya citizen aged 20 to 34 years.
  • Must have a copy of the Kenyan national ID card.
  • Have a valid certificate of Good Conduct.
  • Have a personal accident insurance cover.
  • Have a copy of the KRA PIN certificate.
  • Applicants must not have taken part in an internship program before.
