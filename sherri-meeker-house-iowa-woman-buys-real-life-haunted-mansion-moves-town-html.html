An Iowa woman took her haunted house on a real-life ghost tour this week.

Sherri Meeker recently bought the 150-year-old Newton property, dubbed 'The Haunted Mansion of Jasper County' by locals because of its marked reputation for being occupied by evil spirits.

But because of weak ground conditions, she decided to uproot the wooden structure, which resembles Freddie Krueger's favorite haunt in A Nightmare on Elm Street, and on Tuesday moved it to another, more sturdy, part of town.

But this week, neighbors were wondering if the ghosts would up-sticks with the house, which took dozens of axles and four hours to transport the 100 tons just five miles, or stick around.

A woman called Mindy Dooley posted a suitably creepy video of the mansion, which she says her aunt and uncle owned for more than two decades, on YouTube in 2010.

The minute-and-a-half clip shows images of the house, both inside and out set to an eery soundtrack, and demonstrates, even without the ghouls, the huge job Meeker has taken on in restoring the property to its original grandeur.
