Florida man arrested for torturing federally protected great blue heron to death in front of son

May 30, 2024
blog

A Florida man has been arrested for torturing a federally protected great blue heron to death by cutting off the birds legs and slitting its throat all in front of his distraught son.

The man, Fritz Eugene, 67, was on a fishing trip on March 31 when he became irate that the bird ate his fishing bait. 

He then snatched the bird - breaking its leg in the process -  and continued the heinous act by 'cutting the birds leg off' and 'slitting its throat' at his sister's home in St. Port Lucie - all in front of his horrified son, according to an arrest affidavit obtained by DailyMail.com.

The St. Lucie Police Department were sent to the home- located on Southeast Petunia Avenue - on Sunday night after a domestic dispute broke out.

Eugene's wife told officers she first learned about the innocent bird's killing when she saw a photo of her husband holding the dead bird on her daughter's cell phone, according to the police report. 

When confronted by officers, Eugene said he had no recollection of the bird or the incident and continued denying his involvement although an officer said there was a photo. 

 Eugene is now facing multiple charges including aggravated animal cruelty and cruelty towards a child,  ABC13 News reported.

Blue herons are protected federally under the US Migratory Brid Treaty Act, as well as the Florida Endangered and Threatened Species Rule 

Blue herons are protected federally under the US Migratory Brid Treaty Act and the Florida Endangered and Threatened Species Rule.

The majestic birds weigh only five to six pounds. 

When Fritz's wife called police she told them she was concerned for her safety.

 She told officers she and her husband had an argument and he told her, 'he knew people who could take care of her,' according to an arrest affidavit.

The wife told officers that Fritz had taken fish home before and had killed the fish in front of the children before his most recent killing of the great blue heron. He told his wife his reason for killing the bird was that he was 'angry with it' for killing his fish bait, as per the report, WPTV reported.

During an interview with the couple's children, Eugene's daughter told police that he when he picked her up from school the bird was with him. She said the bird was still alive but had a broken leg at the time. 

Investigators believe the bird's leg was broken when Eugene grabbed it.

Police also said Eugene's son was left 'distraught' by the ordeal, the Post reported. 

Cindy Dionne, the clinical director for Treasure Coast Wildlife, a 501(c)3 nonprofit, told the news outlet that violence toward wildlife, including blue herons, isn't unheard of in this area.

Dionne, who takes care of many of the animals that come into her organization, said, 'You almost desensitize from it.''

She added: 'There's no reason that they should be harming these animals. They're not posing any threat to them. They're actually just in a body of water fishing and trying to catch their next meal.'
