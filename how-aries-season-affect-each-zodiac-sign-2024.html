The snow is melting, the birds are singing, and the sun stays out longer with each passing day. Because Aries is the first sign in the zodiac wheel, it always wipes the slate clean and empowers you to embrace new beginnings. Who cares if you broke your New Year’s resolutions back in January? Everyone knows the real “new year” takes place when the sun enters Aries on March 19 at exactly 11:06 p.m. ET. Prepare to feel harder, better, faster, and stronger, because this season is jam-packed with chaos and change this year.

Aries is a zodiac sign that craves immediate results, but as a Venus-Saturn conjunction in Pisces arrives on March 21, you may be forced to slow-down, especially in your relationships. This conjunction could create delays and frustrations in love, but it’s ultimately asking you to be practical and committed with your heart. Instant gratification rarely ever produces the desired result.

This year, Aries season may feel dreamy, restless, and somewhat bewildering. After all, Mars—Aries’ ruling planet—enters Pisces on March 22, tempering your concern with ego and your desire for action. Mars is the planet of drive and aggression, and Pisces contains an ocean of emotion right below the surface. Will you get swept up in its currents or can you paddle your way to shore?

The intensity of Aries season reaches a boiling point on March 25, when a full moon (and penumbral lunar eclipse) rises at 5 degrees of Libra. This eclipse will join forces with the South Node of Karma, and in astrology, the South Node represents everything you are letting go of and outgrowing. This eclipse may kickstart the process of shedding your skin and creating space for something new, especially in your relationships. And because eclipses evoke major life changes, you may be letting go of something for good.

However, these changes may be confusing to process at first, because on April 1, Mercury stations retrograde at 27 degrees Aries. This year, April Fool’s Day is taking on a whole new meaning, so expect the pranks and hijinks to be next level. This retrograde could intensify your desire to take action, but anger and impulsivity could cause you to major mistakes that could have been avoided. Think twice before you act, especially when Mercury is moving backwards. Because Mercury retrograde tends to increase confusion and resurrect unfinished business, you may not be sure which changes are permanent and which are temporary just yet.

Prepare for an explosion of magic and romance by April 3, when Venus joins forces with mystical and empathetic Neptune. Because Venus will be in Pisces—the zodiac sign of its exaltation—this is a beautiful moment to tell someone you love them, take someone out on a date, or even reunite with a past lover. The energy is perfect for a love that feels out-of-this-world and too good to be true, but remember—feelings are not facts.

Take advantage of this whimsy, as love is bound to get more complicated when Venus enters Aries on April 5. Because Venus is in “detriment” when moving through Aries, it will increase the odds of self-oriented behavior, such as ghosting during conversations, coming on too strong, and pursuing someone that isn’t receptive to you. However, it’s also a beautiful time to indulge in self-love and take dating a lot less seriously, especially because Mercury will be retrograde.

The most climactic moment of this season arrives on April 8, when a total solar eclipse takes place at exactly 19 degrees Aries. This eclipse could evoke cataclysmic changes that are ultimately healing for your identity and your overall sense of independence. Chiron will also be sitting at exactly 19 degrees Aries, causing this eclipse to trigger your deepest wounds and show you how far you’ve come. This eclipse can be thought of as a “teaching moment,” as you will be remembering your own pain and showing others how to navigate theirs. By being vulnerable and true to yourself, you are already helping the world become a better place. Because the North Node of Destiny is currently moving through Aries, this eclipse will push you toward a future that was always meant to be yours. Trust in the process.

These eclipses could rearrange your entire life and kickstart massive transformations, but things may not be moving as fast as you’d like them to. After all, Mars will form a conjunction with Saturn—planet of discipline, restriction, and delayed gratification—which could leave you feeling stuck between a rock and a hard place. Let’s not forget that Mars and Saturn are the most difficult planets of astrology, throwing obstacles in your path and testing your limits. You may want to charge ahead, but this year, Aries season is also about slowing down and forming a game-plan.
