Career and Professional Life

Bukayo Saka was previously able to play for the youth teams of Watford. And also joined Arsenal’s Hale End academy when he was 7 years old. And, after turning 17, he received a professional contract by Arsenal and was promoted to the under-23 side.

In the year 2018, he was able to make his senior competitive debut for Arsenal in their Europa League match against Vorskla Poltava. Then in December 2018, Bukayo was able to make his first full home debut for Arsenal in their Europa League match against Qarabağ.

Likewise, he was able to make his debut on 1 January 2019 in the Premier League. He was able to become the first player born in the 21st century to play in a Premier League match.

Moreover, Bukayo was rewarded for his efforts in Germany with his first Premier League start, in a 3–2 home win over Aston Villa. Following injuries to Sead Kolašinac and Kieran Tierney, he started to establish himself as a regular in the Arsenal first team at left-back.

More About Career

Bukayo Saka was able to sign a new long-term contract with Arsenal on 1 July 2020. He also was able to score his first Premier League goal for the club against Wolverhampton Wanderers, with a half-volley beating goalkeeper Rui Patrício, in a 2–0 win.

In August of the year 2020, he was in the starting 11 and registered an assist in the 2020 FA Community Shield, in which Arsenal clinched a 5–4 victory over Liverpool in the penalty shoot-out after the match was 1–1 after 90 minutes.

Saka also was able to become the Player of the Month on Arsenal and the Player of the Month again in January.

In March 2021, he was able to make his 50th Premier League appearance for Arsenal in a 1–1 draw against Burnley. He also is the second-youngest player in club history to reach that milestone.

And, due to his fine performance, he became the Europa League Player of the Week and even became on the shortlist for the PFA Young Player of the Season.

Likewise, he was able to represent England at the under-16, under-17, under-18, under-19, and under-21 levels. Also, in May 2018, he was included in the England under-17 squad as they hosted the 2018 UEFA European Under-17 Championship.

Moreover, in September 2020, he was able to make his debut for England U21s during a 6–0 victory away to Kosovo. In June of the year 2021, he became the 26-man squad for UEFA Euro 2020.
