Tom Osinde: Blow-By-Blow Account Leading to Ex-Treasury Official's Death

September 17, 2024

  • Tom Osinde, reportedly left his Nakuru home on Sunday, June 18, and was headed to his Nyamira county home
  • Earlier that day, he had sent one of his male employees to take cash to an unnamed woman in Kericho at an unnamed petrol station
  • Few days after his family reported that he was missing, his high end car was found abandoned and vandalised in Sagawa area in Narok county
  • On Wednesday, June 28, police arrested his two employees for further probing, only for his decomposing body to be discovered in River Kuja, Migori county on Thursday, June 29

On Sunday, June 18, a distressed family of former National Treasury employee Tom Osinde reported that he was missing as he was on his way to Nyansiongo, Nyamira county home.

Tom Osinde goes missing

Thursday, June 22, police reported, the public had notified them of an abandoned vandalised high end car they had spotted in Sigawa in Trans Mara, Narok county.

Police officers confirmed the car, indeed belonged to the missing person and had its battery and radio stolen. The thieves left behind some documents which aided police in the search mission.

Tom Osinde's Journey to Nyamira

Osinde was reported to be travelling to Nyamira from Nakuru on the fateful day, only to disappear without a trace on that Sunday, June 18.

According to one of his employees, identified as Julius Mugoi, his boss had given him some cash to deliver to a woman in Kericho. The delivery was done at a petrol station.

Mugoi told journalists that his boss described his attire to the woman for easy identification via phone.

"When I arrived at the petrol station, the woman came to pick the money and informed my boss that she had received it. I then reminded Osinde of my three days leave and he okayed. I proceeded to Kisii for three days and only found out he had disappeared when I returned," Mugoi said.

Another employee, Jacob Khaemba, said he was not home when his boss left, further explaining that Osinde was not a lover of social joints and hardly, if at all, brought strangers home.

On Wednesday, June 28, police officers arrested two employees working in Osinde's Rongai, Nakuru county home after linking them to his disappearance, only a day later to find the missing person's decomposing body in River Kuja, Migori county.

It is a sad tale of a family's agony in search of their loved one who left without a word, only to be found dead, abandoned and decomposing in a River in a county that is neither his native home nor his current home.

The Thursday, June 29, announcement left Kenyans with many questions, each trying to figure out what could have led to the killing of the former staff at the exchequer.
