10 best new black comedians that you will find funny to watch

February 17, 2024
blog

When you initiate a discourse on black comedians, you will likely hear names like Dave Chappelle, Chris Rock, and Maya Rudolph. The legendary and celebrated comedians have been the face of black comedy for years and went on to diversify their art into different factions of entertainment. However, a fresh crop of new black comedians has been growing over the last decade. The comedians are not only energetic but also versatile and know how to take advantage of technology and adopt modern-day trends.

Social media and the internet have greatly contributed to developing new comedians. With just a phone and good internet, many of the budding Gen Z and millennial comedians start making their short skits and comedy videos, and within no time, they go viral.

10 best new black comedians

Becoming a comedian is hard. You must be creative and connect with your audience. Even so, going viral is not a walk in the park. It can take a while before the intended audience takes note of the internet comedians and their videos. Below is a list of new black comedians to look out for, according to Spot Covery:

1. Marie Faustin

Marie Faustin is one of the new black female comedians taking the New York comedy scene by storm. She became the host of Oxygen's Very Real Talk in 2016 and was selected New Face of Comedy at the international Just For Laughs Comedy Festival in Montreal in 2017.

Vulture magazine named the New York-based comedian "Comedian You Should Know" in 2018. Apart from comedy, she also co-hosts the weekly podcast The Unofficial Expert and the weekly comedy showcase Comedy at The Knitting Factory in Brooklyn, NY.

2. Charles Brockman III

With a combined following of more than 11 million followers across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube, Charles Brockman III is one of those young black stand-up comedians you have probably watched on your feed.

The comedian, actor, and vlogger is known for his lifestyle and comical videos, which he uploads on every social media platform. The 24-year-old started his YouTube channel in 2016 and has gradually grown his net worth and fanbase with his comedy.

3. Rachel Pegram

The LA-based comedian is known for hosting her weekly comedy show Skin Contact at Bar Covell in Los Feliz. She is also a writer and actor, appearing on shows like Full Frontal with Samantha Bee, Love Life starring Anna Kendrick, The Other Two, and Comedy Central's Alternatino.

Her work has led to recognition from entities like Comedy Central, which selected her as an Up Next Variety comedian in 2018. Rachel Pegram has also participated in the New Face Characters showcase at the famed Just For Laughs Festival in Montreal in 2018.

4. Jeff Wright

The 29-year-old is one of those black comedians on social media known for his viral online skits, social commentary, and observational comedy. Jeff Wright started stand-up comedy in high school and later moved on to making skits at his home in Orlando before the internet stardom.

His comedy caught the attention of show hosts and other industry bigwigs and soon allowed him to work with renowned shows like Late Night with Seth Meyers, The Amber Ruffin Show, and Celebrity Game Face.

5. Christian Haynes

Popularly known as Thee BlackBadger, Christian Haynes is on the list of new black male comedians you have the internet to thank. The 26-year-old has perfected the art of making relatable comedy videos touching on different life aspects.

He is also great with short skits. The University of North Carolina at Charlotte posted his first video on YouTube in 2019, but it was not until 2020 that he started getting recognition for his comedy videos.

6. Ayo Edebiri

Born in 1995 in Boston, Massachusetts, Ayo Edebiri is one of the fastest-growing comedians of her generation. The 28-year-old is also a writer, producer, actress, and voice actress. She has appeared on Comedy Central's Up Next and co-hosts the podcast Iconography with Olivia Craighead.

Some movies and TV shows she has been featured on include The Bear, Bottoms (2023), Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem (2023), Abbott Elementary, and Theater Camp.

7. Jamel Johnson

Jamel Johnson is an up-and-coming black comedian from Woodbridge, Virginia. He came into the limelight while creating the television show Roc, a comedy-drama about a dedicated man supporting his extended family.

Over the years, Jamel Johnson has created a loyal fanbase that adores him for his comedy, which revolves around racism, law, family, and everything in between.

8. Kenice Mobley

She is a stand-up comedian and filmmaker whose recent accomplishments include debuting on The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon. Vulture listed Kenice Mobley among Comedians You Should and Will Know in 2021, while she performed at the Netflix is A Joke Festival in 2022.

Outside of her stand-up comedy, Kenice Mobley currently works with the Creators Program at Comedy Central. She is also great at hosting, hosting Complexify on ViceNews, Love About Town, a relationship podcast, and Make Yourself Cry.

9. Jaboukie Young-White

Mononymously known as Jaboukie, 29-year-old Jaboukie Young-White has the internet to thank for his rising star. The comic started on social media, particularly X, formerly Twitter, and Instagram, where he posted amusing and relatable videos for his followers.

Today, the talented comedian has done stand-up and written for Big Mouth, among other shows. He starred in Disney's Strange World and is a correspondent for The Daily Show. He is also featured in movies like Someone Great, C’mon C’mon, and Dating and New York.

10. Josh Johnson

33-year-old Josh Johnson is one of the black male comedians on Netflix. He was named New York's Funniest Stand-Up at the New York Comedy Festival in 2018. In the same year, he appeared on the Netflix stand-up comedy series The Comedy Lineup.
