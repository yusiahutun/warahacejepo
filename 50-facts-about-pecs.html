FAQs

1. How do I get to Pécs?

There are several options to get to Pécs. The city has its own airport with flights from various European destinations. Alternatively, you can take a train or bus from Budapest, which is approximately 3 hours away.

2. What are the must-visit attractions in Pécs?

Some of the must-visit attractions in Pécs include the Pécs Cathedral, the Zsolnay Cultural Quarter, the Mosque of Pasha Qasim, and the Early Christian Necropolis. Don’t forget to also explore the charming streets of the old town.

3. Are there any outdoor activities in Pécs?

Yes, there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in Pécs. You can explore the Mecsek Mountains and go hiking or cycling, visit Lake Pécs for water sports and relaxation, or simply take a leisurely stroll in one of the city’s many parks.

4. What is the best time to visit Pécs?

The best time to visit Pécs is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is pleasant and the tourist crowds are smaller. However, each season offers its own unique charm, so it ultimately depends on your personal preferences.

5. Where can I try traditional Hungarian cuisine in Pécs?

Pécs is known for its delicious Hungarian cuisine. You can try traditional dishes at local restaurants, such as goulash, paprika chicken, and chimney cake. Don’t forget to pair your meal with a glass of Hungarian wine!
