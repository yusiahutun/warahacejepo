Hollywood power couple Jodie Turner-Smith and Joshua Jackson shocked fans on Monday when it was revealed they are set to divorce after four years of marriage. 

British actress Jodie - who shares three-year-old daughter Juno Rose Diana with Canadian star Josh - cited 'irreconcilable differences' when she made the filing, with their date of separation listed as September 13.

The move came as a shock to fans, as the couple had looked loved-up just one day before Jodie made the bombshell filing, with the pair holding hands at the COS Autumn/Winter 2023 event on September 12. 

Jodie, 37, and Joshua, 45, have yet to comment publicly on their separation but the actress stated in court filings that she does not want spousal support for either party and the pair do not have a prenup in place.

Canadian actor Joshua is said to have a net worth of $8 million, reports celebritynetworth.com. 

He became a household name when he starred as Pacey Witter in the hit teen show, Dawson's Creek, from 1998 to 2003. 

The Critics' Choice Award nominee went on to land starring roles in Fox's sci-fi series Fringe and Showtime's drama, The Affair. 

Most recently, Joshua took on the leading role in Paramount+ series Fatal Attraction, based on the 1987 film of the same name. 

The father-of-one previously revealed that being paid so much in his younger years gave him imposter syndrome. 

Reflecting on being cast in Dawson's Creek at the age of 19, he confessed: 'I would make more money doing four episodes of Dawson's Creek than most of my friends' parents made in a year..

'That was the center of my imposter syndrome: You're trying to set me up to be a role model to kids, and I definitely am not, and you're paying me all this money that I don't deserve.'

Joshua and Jodie reside in the Hollywood star's former childhood home in Topanga, California, after he bought the property in 2001.

The actor was born in Vancouver but raised in Los Angeles until he was eight years old, at which point his parents divorced and he returned to the Canadian city with his mother and sister.

Speaking about his decision to buy his childhood home, he told Mr Porter: ' My father unfortunately was not a good father or a husband and exited the scene, but that house in Topanga was where everything felt simple, so it was a very healing thing for me to do.'

Joshua previously owned a home in West Hollywood with ex-girlfriend Diane Kruger which he sold in 2017 for $5.76 million a year after they split.

While it appears neither Joshua nor Jodie will be seeking spousal support, their living and child custody arrangements have yet to be announced. 

Jodie has asked for joint custody of their three-year-old daughter in the divorce and is being represented by celebrity divorce lawyer Laura Wasser. 

Laura has handled a number of high-profile, high-net-worth dissolutions, including those for Angelina Jolie, Heidi Klum, Kim Kardashian, Johnny Depp and Kevin Costner. 

As Joshua and Jodie did not sign a prenuptial agreement, they will now have to come to an agreement surrounding their divorce settlement. 

A pre-nuptial agreement is essentially a contract drawn up by a couple before they get married. Typically it will list all of the assets each person owns, including any debts, and specifies how these will be divided up in the event of a divorce. 

The extent to which a pre-nup is enforced can depend on the state the couple reside in. While all 50 states officially recognize the agreement, many disagree on the specifics. 

Rachael Burns, who founded True Worth Financial Planning, told DailyMail.com that she believes all engaged couples should sign a prenup regardless of wealth or status. 

She revealed: 'Pre-nuptial agreements get this reputation for being for really wealthy people. And they are seen as this very unromantic thing. But I think it's actually very romantic. It's a way of avoiding so much potential conflict.'
