The Cozy Sneakers That Sell Out Every Winter Are Back in Stock

March 27, 2024
blog

As a young fashion writer in my 20s, I didn’t own sneakers; I envisioned myself a Carrie Bradshaw type, running around town in my 5-inch stilettos. Then one fateful evening almost 10 years ago, I broke my ankle, and my sky-high shoe dreams went down the drain. Overnight, I became obsessed with finding every single pair of comfortable yet fashionable sneakers I could get my hands on. I’ve practically become a sneaker expert since — and right now, Cariuma is the brand to watch.

The brand has racked up an impressive number of fans, including fashion editors and celebrities like Alexandra Daddario. That’s because something it does well (apart from providing your feet with supreme comfort) is upgrade its shoes in new colors every season, from Pantone-inspired spring hues and vibrant neons to fun bandana prints. But it’s the cozy Therma Suede Sneaker that truly has my attention right now, thanks to three new colors that are absolutely made for crisp fall and winter days — and it’s bound to sell out.

The high-top sneakers are a thermal version of Cariuma’s iconic OCA High Sneakers, designed with — you guessed it — thermal lining. Well, vegan shearling lining, to be exact, but you get the point. This winter, they’re available in three new colors — olive-brown, light gray, and black with a contrast white rubber sole. This particular style sells out every winter thanks to its water-repellent vegan suede exterior and that aforementioned plush lining, which helps keep feet warm below 32-degrees Fahrenheit, per the brand.

They’re also designed with recycled materials, a comfortable (and removable!) bio-foam insole, and a natural rubber sole. The silhouette is closer to a high-top sneaker than a typical boot, which gives it a certain je ne sais quoi once those icy temperatures roll around. They come in sizes 5 to 13, with half sizes available, and are $149.

In case you haven’t quite hopped on the Cariuma bandwagon yet, maybe the fact that they plant trees for each pair of shoes purchased will convince you. And, yes, emphasis on trees — because, for each pair sold, they plant two trees in the Brazilian rainforest to help aid in restoration and preservation. Why not do a little good while you stay warm this winter?

I never thought I’d be grateful that I broke my ankle — or that I’d be looking forward to freezing temperatures — but, thanks to the Cariuma Therma Suede Sneakers, I actually want to wear practical shoes this winter. If you’re as curious about the comfy kicks as I am, snap them up before they inevitably sell out (again).
