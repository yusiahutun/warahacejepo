16 Best Mother's Day Songs

blog

It’s nearly impossible to succinctly identify the impact our parents have had on us. So as Mother’s Day quickly approaches, we’ve rounded up the best songs to express that very specific gratitude and love for mom. From the voices and stories of Bruce Springsteen and Adele, to Fleetwood Mac and Tupac, there’s something destined to fit whatever sort of relationship you share with your own mother this spring.

“Sweetest Devotion” by Adele

Of all singers of the 21st century, there are few who can emote musically the way Adele does. On “Sweetest Devotion,” she sings loudly of an undying love—in this case, it’s the love for her newborn son. Flip it on its head, and it's just as appropriate as an appreciation track for mom.

Download

“Rhiannon” by Fleetwood Mac

“Rhiannon” is a song about an enchanting, powerful witch/goddess figure, which is pretty much exactly what every mother is.

Download

“***Flawless” by Beyoncé feat. Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

In a ringing celebration of the complexity of womanhood, Beyoncé breaks down not only why being a mother and a wife is beautiful, but also why it’s just as spectacular to be a woman regardless.

Download

“Mama” by The Spice Girls

“Mama” by the Spice Girls breaks down the glorious metamorphosis of mothers switching from foe to friend as we grow up. The verses capture that very specific teen angst (“She used to be my only enemy and never let me free / Catching me in places that I knew I shouldn't be”) that eventually switches to a much more mature admiration ( “So now, I see through your eyes / All that you did was love”).

Download

“Promise to Try” by Madonna

“Promise To Try” is a cutting, uncharacteristically somber Madonna song, as she sings of her late mother, promising to carry on her memory.

Download

“Hey Mamma” by Kanye West

With a childish rasp in his voice and an entire choir backing him up, “Hey Mamma” sounds like the world’s best homemade card. “Hey mama / I want to scream so loud for you / ‘Cause I'm so proud of you,” Kanye sings out on this wholesome, beaming cut from early in his catalogue.

Download

“The Wish” by Bruce Springsteen

Like most Bruce Springsteen tracks, “The Wish” has a story arc worthy of its own Lifetime movie. Fit for both Mother’s Day and Christmas, it’s a deeper cut worthy of some more attention, as Bruce chronicles his own life and upbringing through events centered around the guitar his mother gave to him as a child for the holidays.

Download

“Dear Mama” by Tupac

In an era best known for gangsta rap’s perceived “hardness,” Tupac’s “Dear Mama” shone through as an emotional and vulnerable plot-heavy guiding light. While “Dear Mama” breaks down Pac’s own upbringing, the track is a timeless ode to any and all hard working mothers.

Download

“The Best Day” by Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift carves out a cut for mom on 2008’s Fearless. It’s classic vintage Swift, as her acoustic guitar strums lead her through some of her best mom-related memories in every pivotal stage of life.

Download

“letter 2 my momma” by 21 Savage

This track, boasting the rapper's signature drawl laced with his trademark “straight up” ad-libs, is an incredibly 21 Savage way to pay homage to your mother.

Download

“Oh Mother” by Christina Aguilera

“Oh mother, we’re stronger / From all the tears you have shed / Oh mother, don’t look back, ‘cause he’ll never hurt us again,” Aguilera reassures in the chorus, as she candidly speaks about her mother’s experiences with domestic violence and praises her resilience.

Download

“Behold A Lady” by Outkast

Though it seems safe to assume Andre 3000 wasn’t writing this one about his own mom, “Behold A Lady” is a song that celebrates strong, sophisticated, and complex women. So feel free to bend this track to honor the one who raised you.

Download

“Mother” by Pink Floyd

In the way only Pink Floyd could, “Mother” distorts and magnifies the ideas and tendencies of an overprotective mother and a son seeking validation through the eyes of a character named Pink. “Momma’s gonna keep you right here, under her wing / She won’t let you fly, but she might let you sing,” he sings, melodically and lyrically nodding to the “hush little baby” lullaby.

Download

“Look What You’ve Done” by Drake

Flipping the oft-aggressive phrase “Look What You’ve Done,” Drake chronicles all of the sacrifices his mother has made for him in his life—and all of the ways he’s proud to pay her back now: “I got to make good on my promise / It all worked out, girl, we shoulda known / ‘Cause you deserve it.”

Download

“Be Yourself” by Frank Ocean

“Be Yourself” is actual audio of Frank’s childhood friend’s mother nagging at him about the dangers of drugs and alcohol for a sustained minute and nearly 30 seconds, which may be the realest, most widely relatable mother-related content of all.

Download

“A Song For Mama” by Boyz II Men

Rounding out their run in the '90s, “A Song For Mama” is a perfectly polished R&B track from Philly’s finest boy group.

Download
