NFL Draft Grades 2024: How strong is the 2024 class and what are the top picks profile?

April 14, 2024
blog

The 2024 NFL Draft is poised to captivate football fans and franchises alike with a particularly strong class, heralded for its outstanding quarterbacks and skill players. At the forefront is USC's Caleb Williams, the dynamic 2022 Heisman Trophy winner known for his dual-threat capabilities.

Hot on his heels is Marvin Harrison Jr. from Ohio State, a remarkable talent who could have topped the pass catchers in the 2023 draft, now returning as a junior to potentially become the top wide receiver picked

The top 20 prospects in this year's NFL Draft class

1. QB Caleb Williams, USC

2022 Heisman Trophy winner known for his electrifying play as a dual-threat quarterback. Williams is considered a franchise-altering talent, potentially worthy of the No. 1 overall pick.

2. WR Marvin Harrison Jr., Ohio State

With NFL Hall of Fame bloodlines, Harrison combines size, speed, strength, and a sharp football IQ. He's expected to be a WR1 from the day he is drafted.

3. QB Drake Maye, North Carolina

Known for his strong arm, Maye's decision-making and ball placement need improvement. He remains a top prospect with first-round and top-10 potential.

4. WR Malik Nabers, LSU

Exceptional at changing direction and accelerating, Nabers is an open-field threat both in the slot and on the outside.

5. T Joe Alt, Notre Dame

Alt combines size, strength, and a high football IQ, making him a likely top-10 pick as a future long-term tackle.

6. WR Rome Odunze, Washington

Odunze stands out even in a talented offensive unit and is seen as a potential WR1, justifying a top-10 pick.

7. TE Brock Bowers, Georgia

Though slightly smaller, Bowers' versatility and reliable blocking make him an ultimate offensive weapon with All-Pro potential.

8. CB Cooper DeJean, Iowa

DeJean's versatility allows him to play effectively at cornerback, safety, or slot, characterized by excellent footwork and athleticism.

9. DI Byron Murphy II, Texas

A versatile lineman who excels in both run defense and pass rushing, Murphy is projected as a first-round impact starter.

10. CB Quinyon Mitchell, Toledo

Mitchell's advanced coverage metrics are among the best PFF has recorded, showing first-round potential.

11. DI Jer'zhan Newton, Illinois

Effective in multiple alignments, Newton's hand usage and pass-rush tools compensate for physical limitations.

12. T Taliese Fuaga, Oregon State

Known for his powerful play and mental toughness, Fuaga has the traits to succeed at tackle or guard at the NFL level.

13. T Troy Fautanu, Washington

An athlete with the skills suited for a zone-blocking scheme, Fautanu shows promise in multiple positions on the line.

14. CB Nate Wiggins, Clemson

His athleticism and competitiveness are indisputable, predicting a long-term role as a starting cornerback.

15. CB Terrion Arnold, Alabama

While raw, Arnold's athletic prowess gives him a high ceiling in both man and zone cover schemes.

16. ED Dallas Turner, Alabama

A potential first-round edge rusher, Turner's strengths lie in pass-rushing, with room to grow in run defense.

17. ED Jared Verse, Florida State

Known for his work ethic and strength, Verse is poised to make an impact despite some physical limitations.

18. T Olumuyiwa Fashanu, Penn State

One of the youngest prospects, Fashanu has outstanding potential and athleticism, deemed a first-round talent.

19. C Jackson Powers-Johnson, Oregon

Powers-Johnson brings a combination of power and finesse to either center or guard positions, marking him as a top talent.

20. ED Laiatu Latu, UCLA

Although not the most athletic, Latu's effective hand usage and pass-rushing skills make him a solid pick.

How good is NFL 2024 draft class?

Like every year, there are players who stand out and remain in the NFL as the best in the league; However, this year's class stands out for having some of the best QB prospects of the last 10 years.

Caleb Williams is one of the biggest and is the standout of this year's class. He is projected to be drafted by the Chicago Bears and it'll be interesting to see how he performs next season.
