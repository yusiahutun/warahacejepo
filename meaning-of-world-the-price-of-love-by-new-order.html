Meaning of World (the Price Of Love) by New Order

April 10, 2024
blog

The New Order song titled 'World (the Price Of Love)' is a mesmerizing piece that delves into the complexities of love and its impact on the world. At first glance, one might interpret the song as a commentary on the price we pay for love. However, a closer examination reveals a plethora of unexpected and imaginative themes that contribute to the overall message of the song.Let's begin by analyzing the standout lyrics in the song and exploring the themes associated with each:1. "I turn sideways to the Sun, Keep my thoughts from everyone" - This lyric suggests a desire for solitude and introspection. It implies a longing to detach from the outside world, emphasizing the importance of personal growth and self-reflection in the quest for love.Theme: The pursuit of love requires a balance between individual introspection and connecting with others.2. "And when this building is on fire, These flames can't burn any higher" - Here, the metaphorical reference to a burning building signifies a state of chaos and destruction. It symbolizes the intensity and volatility of love, where emotions can become overwhelming and uncontrollable.Theme: Love can ignite passion and intensity, but if not handled with care, it can lead to destruction.3. "That's the price of love (that's the price of love), Can you feel it? (can you feel it?)" - This recurring chorus introduces the central theme of the song. It explores the emotional and sometimes physical sacrifices one must make to experience the depths of love.Theme: Love demands sacrifice and vulnerability, but the rewards can be immeasurable.4. "If we could buy it now, How long will it last?" - This lyric poses a thought-provoking question about the transient nature of love. It proposes the idea of purchasing love, suggesting that if it were possible, one might wonder how long it would truly endure.Theme: Love is ephemeral and cannot be commodified or sustained indefinitely.5. "That's the price of love (that's the price of love), If we could buy it now, How long will it last?" (Repeated multiple times) - This repetition reinforces the central theme of the song. It emphasizes the idea that love comes at a cost, and even if it could be bought, its longevity is uncertain.Theme: Love cannot be easily obtained or guaranteed.As we examine these individual themes, we begin to see a larger narrative emerging - one that suggests love is a transformative force that requires courage, self-reflection, sacrifice, and an acceptance of its inherent unpredictability. The song encourages listeners to contemplate the multifaceted aspects of love and recognize that its true price cannot be measured in material terms.Furthermore, 'World (the Price Of Love)' challenges conventional notions of love. It invites us to explore the complexities and contradictions that arise when we pursue deep connections with others. The song suggests that love is not meant to be easily grasped or controlled but rather experienced in all its vibrant and elusive forms.In conclusion, New Order's 'World (the Price Of Love)' is more than just a song about the cost one pays for love. It is a profound exploration of the human experience, delving into themes of introspection, sacrifice, intensity, transience, and the enigmatic nature of love itself. Through its poetic and imaginative lyrics, the song sparks contemplation and invites listeners to embark on a journey of self-discovery and introspection. So, take a moment, immerse yourself in its melodies, and reflect on the profound themes that lie within.
