Understanding Power of Attorney

Before we dive into the specifics of Importance of Power of Attorney Form in Utah, it’s important to have a basic understanding of what power of attorney means. Power of attorney is a legal document that gives someone else the power to act on your behalf. This person is known as your “agent” or “attorney-in-fact.” The power of attorney document outlines the types of decisions your agent can make and when they can make them.

There are two main types of power of attorney: general and specific. A general power of attorney gives your agent broad powers to act on your behalf, while a specific power of attorney limits their authority to specific decisions or situations.

It’s important to note that a power of attorney document only remains valid while you are alive. If you were to pass away, the power of attorney would no longer be in effect. At that point, your will and other estate planning documents would come into play.
