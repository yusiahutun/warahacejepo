Darts fans want 'annoying' Dimitri Van den Bergh dance banned from Premier League

May 24, 2024
blog

Dimitri Van den Bergh marked his return to Premier League darts with a 6-2 win over Jonny Clayton in Belfast – but his performance didn’t go down well with some fans.

The Belgian was one of the wildcard picks as the PDC finally confirmed the line-up for annual darting roadshow at the start of the week. It wasn’t an entirely popular choice, with the likes Luke Humphries, Joe Cullen and Ross Smith missing out.

The world number nine went someway to justifying his selection with a clinical victory over 2021 winner Clayton. However, he has already started “annoying” some fans after kicking off the night with his trademark dance routine on the stage to the Pharrell Williams hit ‘Happy’.

It seemed like some fans in attendance had already taken against Van den Bergh as a few boos could be heard in the arena during his walk-on.

One surprised fans tweeted: “Surely Dimitri Van Den Bergh wasn’t getting booed #PLD23.”

Other fans hit out at the 28-year-old’s dancing, with one demanding: “Should put a ban on this clown from dancing when he walks on.” Another said: “The dancing is the only reason he’s in the PL.”

One user commented: “This is gonna get very annoying, very fast. Not sure my stomach could take this every Thursday.”

Another fan, presumably referring to Peter Wright, added: “Now we’ve got two wallys dancing every week.”

One suggested Van den Bergh should take a leaf out of world champion Michael Smith’s book.

“This (annoying) guy needs attention badly. Too many copycats to steal the show. Just conquer the fans by throwing awesome darts. A good darter doesn't need a dance but a cheer and a smile after 180!!! Take @Michael180Smith for example, just a (world) champ by being himself.”

Van den Bergh's victory over Clayton booked a semi-final on the night against Michael van Gerwen, who defeated Smith 6-3 in their quarter-final.
