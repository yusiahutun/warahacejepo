How to Make Your Music Sound Better

August 06, 2024
blog

EQ can help you improve the sound of your headphones with little effort. You can make the sound clearer in the spots you want by reducing or boosting some audio frequencies.

Since EQ is adjustable, you can always alter the settings to get the best sound for what you're listening to. Your headphones might sound good—even great—but what if you could make them even better? Read on to find out how you can do that with EQ!

What Is an Equalizer?

EQ is short for "equalizer," which is the act of reducing or boosting specific frequencies in digital sound. EQ is used both by producers and consumers of music, films, games, audiobooks, and more.

When you're using an EQ as a listener, the EQ becomes a matter of personalization. You can attenuate some imperfect sounds in the music and boost the ones you enjoy, and by doing so, you change how you hear music.

The healthy human ear can hear a wide range of frequencies, from 20Hz to 20,000Hz. Any sort of audio contains a mix of these frequencies. In audio, higher frequency means higher pitch. For instance, women typically have a higher frequency voice compared to men.

When it comes to adjusting an EQ, you'll frequently hear about three terms: low-end, mid-range, and high-end. These are different frequency areas that affect specific aspects of the sound.

  • Low-end: 100-125Hz
  • Mid-range: 250-2,000Hz
  • High-end: 4,000-16,000Hz

All in all, EQ allows you to customize your experience by personally selecting which frequencies you want to hear more and which ones you want to hear less.

What Are the Right EQ Settings for Your Headphones?

Considering what we said about EQ, there's no single perfect EQ setting. Instead, EQ is a matter of taste and of what you're listening to. Still, general EQ settings are a good starting point for customizing them to suit your taste perfectly.

When setting up your EQ, you should do it in a way that brings out the main elements of the sound you want to hear. For instance, for a techno track, it's best to boost up the low-end, add a bit to the high-end, and reduce the mid-range in what's known as a balanced sound. On the other hand, if you're watching a movie and want to catch the dialogues, then the mid-range is what you'd want to boost.

Regardless of EQ being a matter of taste, some settings are generally good for different purposes. Most quality headphones will have apps for EQ. If yours doesn't, you can use third-party equalizer apps.

Without further ado, here are the EQ settings we recommend for different scenarios with your headphones.

Gaming EQ Settings

Gaming is a matter of detecting tiny sounds such as footsteps and enjoying a bit of bass when it comes to gunshots, explosions, car engines, and so on. However, the low-end isn't the focus here since you don't want the bass to distract you from other details.

Music EQ Settings

For music, balanced bass is essential. Tuning up the midranges a little to catch the vocals and boosting the high-end to hear the melody clearly sums up a generally good EQ setting for music.

Movie EQ Settings

When it comes to audio, movies are a mixture of action, ambient sounds, and dialogue. Boosting the low-end for the action, the high-end for the ambient sounds, and the mid-range for dialogue can improve your experiences significantly.

Adjust Your Headphone EQ for Optimum Sound Quality

Your headphones might sound nice with a flat EQ, but once you get into adjusting your sound, you'll never want to go back to the default settings. EQ is a simple adjustment once you get to understand the basics of it. Yet, despite its simplicity, it can drastically change the way you hear sounds from your headphones.

Remember, though, that different music, games, and movies have unique qualities, and what sounds great in one game might be awful in another.
