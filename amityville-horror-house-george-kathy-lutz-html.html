What happened to George and Kathy Lutz? The true story about the couple who fled the Amityville Horr

A documentary on George and Kathy Lutz, who ran away from their home and claimed it was plagued with evil spirits, will air tonight at 9pm on BBC Two. 

The pair ran away from their home in Long Island, New York, after believing it to be haunted and inhabited by an intruder. 

Their story was widely believed to be a hoax, but the history behind the Amityville home is very much real. 

So, what happened to George and Kathy Lutz and where are they now? Read on below for everything you need to about the couple and their experiences at the Amityville Horror house. 

Although many believed the couple's (pictured) story was a hoax - the brutal murders that took place in three story colonial home are very much real 

What happened to George and Kathy Lutz?

After moving to the Amityville property in December 1975, George and Kathy Lutz thought they had been able to climb onto the property ladder for a steal of a price. 

Little did they know that they had purchased a house that was the setting of a mass murder less than a year earlier.

The couple moved in with Kathy's three children - which she had from a previous marriage, yet it took just 28 days before they fled the house. 

Explaining why they chose to do so, George said: 'There were…odours in the house that came and went. There were sounds. The front door would slam shut in the middle of the night…I couldn't get warm in the house for many days,' in an interview with ABC News. 

The couple also said there was a strange gelatinous substance on the carpet, with Kathy allegedly transformed into an old woman, while George also even claimed to have seen her levitating.

Spooked by their apparently haunted experience, the family chose to flee the house, leaving all their belongings behind. 

While their claims were never proven, what happened in the building prior to them moving in is certainly true.  

On November 13 1974, Ronald DeFeo Jr went on a brutal killing spree, murdering his entire family in their while they slept, using a 0.35-caliber Marlin lever-action rifle.

DeFeo, who also adopted the nickname Butch, then went to the local bar later that same day, weeping that his two sisters, two brothers, mother and father had all been killed. 

Where are George and Kathy Lutz now? 

It has been over 48 years since Kathy and George fled along with their three children and dog, claiming they were being haunted by evil spirits

After fleeing the house they claimed to be haunted, Kathy and George divorced in 1980, and both have since passed away. 

Kathy died of emphysema in 2004, with George passing away in 2006 after enduring a battle with heart disease.

Their children have tended to shy away from the horror spotlight, most notably their daughter Missy, who has not commented publicly on the matter. 

However, Daniel Lutz appeared in the 2013 documentary My Amityville Horror and maintained the view that the hauntings were not fabricated by his parents.

Another of George and Kathy's children, Christopher Quaratino told the Seattle Times that although the book was not a hoax, it was extremely amplified. 

He had a rocky relationship with stepfather, and chose to move away from the family aged 16.
