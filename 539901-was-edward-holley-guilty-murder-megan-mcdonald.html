Who was Megan McDonald?

Megan was a 20-year-old college student at the time of her death. She lived in Orange County and worked at the Galleria Mall in Middletown. She was the daughter of Betty Jo McDonald and Dennis McDonald.

Her father was a retired NYPD detective who died of a heart attack in 2002, a year before McDonald was killed. He was known for investigating the first World Trade Center bombing in 1993. Megan had an elder sister, Karen Whalen.

What happened to Megan McDonald?

On 15th March 2003, Megan McDonald’s beaten body was found in a field off Bowser Road. At the beginning of the month, she had signed a lease for a new apartment near Middletown, New York. She was looking forward to starting her life as a young adult, not knowing what awaited her.

On 13th March, she began her shift at the American café, working part-time as a waitress. According to NBC News, she worked until 3:00 pm and later went to a nearby bank to withdraw some money.

Finding Megan McDonald

On 14th March, her family and friends started worrying because they hadn't seen or heard from her. Also, she didn't show up at work. They constantly called her cell phone and left voicemails to no answer.

As time passed, their concern over Megan McDonald's missing status grew. However, they didn’t have to wait much longer to find out about her whereabouts. On 15th March, owners of a property in the area of 229 Bowser Road in Wallkill town saw a body.

The individuals knew it looked like a young female and immediately alerted the Middletown State Police. The New York State Police responded and rushed to the scene. Immediately, they identified the body as Megan’s through her driver's license.

Investigators believed that the young lady was murdered at the location where she was found. A few days later, Megan's 1991 Mercury Sable car was seen parked at the Kensington Manor apartment complex. It is alleged that the car was parked as if some people left it in a hurry.

Was Edward Holley found guilty of the murder of Megan McDonald?

According to Times Union, Edward Holley was indicted by an Orange County grand jury on 29th January 2024. Edward's attorney, Paul Weber, entered a not-guilty plea on Edward's behalf of him. He pleaded with the judge to let him remain free as he awaited trial.

Unfortunately, the judge declined his requests and ordered Edward to be held in the Orange County jail. She set the bail at $500,000 cash or $1 million bond. If Edward is convicted of second-degree murder, he will face a maximum of 25 years to life in prison.

Even though the court waived the reading of the full indictment, the hearing was emotional for Megan’s family. Outside the court, her family stated:

For the first time in 21 years, Edward Holley is going to jail for the murder of Megan McDonald — not for any other reason but for the murder of Megan McDonald. And our family is beyond grateful, and we are relieved.
