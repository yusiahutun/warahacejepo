Where to buy an antique or vintage engagement ring - 12 best styles to shop

June 30, 2024
blog

These antique diamond rings are a piece of history!

    Antique and vintage engagement rings are becoming increasingly popular thanks to their uniqueness and sense of nostalgia - but you have to know where to look if you want to find your perfect style.

    There are many wonderful antique jewellery stores all over the UK, as well as some treasure-stocked sellers online where you can find a piece of history to wear on your ring finger. Equally, buying an antique or vintage engagement ring is a stylish way to make a sustainable choice.

    How old are antique and vintage engagement rings?

    Typically, an antique piece is classed as 50 years or older. The most popular styles come from the Edwardian, Victorian and Art Deco periods - with each having its own signatures.

    A vintage piece is generally considered to be 20 to 50 years old. Fancy some eighties bling?

    Penelope Cruz's stunning sapphire engagement ring is an Edwardian style, surrounded with pretty bezel diamonds.

    Princess Kate's engagement ring dates back to the 1980s

    Of course, today's most famous vintage ring owner is arguably the Princess of Wales, whose iconic sapphire and diamond piece dates back to 1981 when Prince Charles purchased it for Princess Diana from British jeweller Garrard. Its inspiration dates back to 1840, however.

    Victorian engagement rings

    In the Victorian era (generally considered to be from 1837 to 1901), ring styles varied a lot, though halos and clusters of diamonds are distinctive settings from this time. Queen Victoria's favourite colour, blue, was often featured, too.
