Country Music Singer/Songwriter Jon Pardi Signs With WME For Global Representation

blog

Country star Jon Pardi has taken a significant step in his career by signing a global representation deal with WME (William Morris Endeavor). The agreement encompasses various aspects of his career, including touring, brand partnerships, TV, film, and digital representation.

“Jon is an incredible talent who has seen a tremendous amount of success through the impact he’s made on country music,” said Joey Lee, Co-Head of WME’s Nashville Office. “We’re excited to have Jon join the WME family and be a part of the journey in taking his career to the next level.”

Pardi has six No. 1 singles, including hits like “Head Over Boots,” “Dirt On My Boots,” “Heartache Medication,” “Last Night Lonely,” “Night Shift,” and “Your Heart or Mine.” His contributions extend beyond performing, as he is recognized for his writing, producing, and singing skills, earning accolades from the ACM (Academy of Country Music) and CMA (Country Music Association).

The pinnacle of Pardi’s recent achievements was his invitation by Alan Jackson invited Pardi to join the Grand Ole Opry at Stagecoach 2023, and he was later inducted by Garth Brooks, solidifying his status as the first artist from California to become a member of the Grand Ole Opry.

Pardi is co-managed by Red Light Management (RLM) and C3 Presents, signed to Capital Records and Kevin Meads at WME.
