Jayson Tatum is the breakout star for the Boston Celtics who led his squad into the NBA Finals 2022 with his best gameplay this year. Ever since he finished with college studies, his career has prospered upwards and so many experts already said that he has the potential to be the best. Whenever required, Jayson is inevitable and has routinely provided outstanding performances.

Early life

Jayson Tatum was born on 3rd March 1998 in St. Louis, Missouri. For both of his parents, he was born when both were 19 years old and studying their undergraduate. At the time, they were not married thus, his mother made the decision to raise him alone. His father, Justin, played basketball at ‘St. Louis University.’ Later, at St. Louis’ ‘Christian Brothers College High School,’ he served as a gym instructor and basketball coach. Jayson and his mother are very close in their personal lives. He even claims that she is the cause of his accomplishments. Harry Giles, a former Duke teammate, and current NBA player is a personal buddy of his. Jayson Tatum’s younger half-brothers and sisters are Jaycob and Kayden Tatum.

His mother graduated from ‘St. Louis University with a law degree and went on to practice law. Due to a lack of financial resources, she struggled to raise Jayson while still an undergraduate student. Jayson has been a basketball player since he was a small youngster. While his father was a basketball player and coach, his godfather was Larry Hughes, an NBA player who was a college and high school friend of his father’s. Jayson grew into a powerful big guy with excellent basketball abilities. His father told him that he was meant to be a basketball player. As a result, Jayson developed an early interest in the sport. At the end of the game, he was up against much bigger and stronger opponents.

Education

Jayson enrolled at ‘Chaminade College Preparatory,’ where he rose quickly through the basketball team’s ranks. He befriended future NBA star Matthew Tkachuk throughout his high school years. Jayson’s basketball career was therefore placed on a good basis. He enrolled at Duke University after graduating from high school.

Jayson Tatum Career

In December of 2016, Jayson made his first public appearance. He scored 10 points in his first game and led his side to a victory over ‘Maine.’ The 2017 ‘NBA Summer League tournament in Utah was his debut appearance with the ‘Celtics.’ Jayson put together an outstanding performance, averaging 18.7 points, 9.7 rebounds, 2.3 steals, and 2.0 assists per game. The Boston Celtics have qualified for the NBA Playoffs for the first time in franchise history. Jayson was instrumental in several of his team’s triumphs during the season. When he scored 28 points against the ‘Philadelphia 76ers’ in the second round, he set an all-time record for a rookie playing for the ‘Celtics’ in a playoff game.
