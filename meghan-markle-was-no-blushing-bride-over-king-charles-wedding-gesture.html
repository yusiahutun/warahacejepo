Meghan Markle was no 'blushing bride' over King Charles' wedding gesture

Meghan Markle had a bold response to King Charles’ moving gesture on the day she married Prince Harry, an author claimed.

On May 19, 2018, Meghan Markle was officially part of The Firm. She exchanged vows with her ‘Haz’ – the cute nickname she likes to give the Duke of Sussex. Prince Harry married the Duchess in a fairytale ceremony in St George’s Chapel five years ago. However, only days before their big day, her dad Thomas Markle dropped out of appearing, over health fears, and subsequently left the Duchess in the lurch. But King Charles swooped in to extend a moving gesture to his future daughter-in-law. Although, it reportedly saw a bold reply from the Suits actor.

Meghan Markle was no ‘blushing bride’ to King Charles’ gesture

To prevent any fears of the big day being ruined or affected by Thomas Markle not being able to make the trip, Charles stepped in. It came after reports from The Mirror of several hiccups leading to the royals‘ wedding day. From a row with Kate Middleton, the Princess of Wales – which Meghan later told Oprah the rumors were the other way round – to reports of tiaras causing trouble.

The former Prince of Wales offered to do one of the most important roles of the wedding itself.

According to Robert Hardman, author of Queen of Our Times, he was honored to offer to escort Meghan down the aisle for the ceremony. But, it’s claimed Meghan didn’t jump for joy at the gesture, and made a different suggestion.

Hardman wrote: “The reply, according to one friend, was not quite what he was expecting: “Can we meet halfway?'” Here was an indicator that this was no blushing bride, but a confident, independent woman determined to make a grand entrance on her own.”

On the day itself, after the royal family arrived in the chapel and took their seats, Meghan appeared around noon. She began walking down the aisle and met King Charles. He escorted her the remainder of the way, through the quire of the chapel.

It’s believed the security for the event was below $3 million. Although the expensive day may seem a hefty sum, it’s said tourists flocking to see the couple in their best outfits brought in $80 million.

Meghan, looking like a princess out of a Disney movie, wore a custom-made Givenchy gown. It had long sleeves and a sweeping train. The classy fashion number also featured a 16-ft silk veil. It was embroidered with 55 flowers to represent the Commonwealth, as well as California and where she lived with Harry.

Queen Elizabeth II loaned a diamond tiara to Meghan, which was made back in 1932 for Queen Mary.

Fans began appearing outside Windsor Castle from 8am on the day. They wanted to get ahead before celebrity guests began arriving 90 minutes later.

After the ceremony, the newly-wed couple walked out of the chapel and kissed on the steps to adoring fans.
