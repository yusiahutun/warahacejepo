A WWE star's real age has been revealed amid his rise to stardom in the company.

The man in question is one of the stars of NXT on Tuesday nights, and has received plaudits for his strength and athleticism in his performances despite his short in-ring career.

Having picked up his first title in WWE at the start of the new year, fans have been left excited about what he can go onto achieve having received a push from the company at such a young age.


It had been thought that the man in question was 22 years-old and therefore the youngest North American Champion ever, overtaking Velveteen Dream, who was 23, but it appears that may not be the case after all.

Oba Femi, the current NXT North American champion, is actually three years older and 25 - that's according to his mother on social media.

WWE star Oba Femi is seemingly 25 year-old rather than 22, as had been billed by company

Femi's mother, meanwhile, has revealed his real age on social media following his title victory

Femi, real name Isaac Odugbesan, shot up the ranks in NXT when he won the men's breakout tournament on January 2, and he cashed in his contract for a championship match a week later, defeating Dragon Lee.

He made history in doing so, becoming the first African-born wrestler to hold the championship and the first person from the WWE's Next In Line programme to win a title.

While fans have been left intrigued by his potential, it was widely reported that Femi was just 22. His mother however, Instagram handle @odugbesanmaria, revealed he is actually 25 in an Instagram conversation in September.

Heights and weights are often manipulated by WWE for effect, though ages generally are now. 

After one user posted 'Oba's only 22?? Damnnn,' Maria Odugbesa corrected them with '25'.

Another said: 'He was born in 2001,' leading to a reply of: 'He is my son darling. I am his mother. Love you.'

It appears the star is indeed three years older than was thought, and was born on April 2 1998.

Fans seem excited by Femi, who has displayed impressive athleticism and power in showings

He is currently enjoying a feud with Dragon Lee (left), who he beat for the title this month

Femi has established himself as a heel in the WWE, cutting his first promo on Tuesday's episode of NXT and closing the North American Championship that former holder Lee had been running.

Lee confronted the 6'4 star, and the two exchanged words, suggesting their feud will continue going forward.
