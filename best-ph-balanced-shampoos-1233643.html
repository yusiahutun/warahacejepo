What Does pH-balanced Mean?

The pH level basically refers to how acidic or alkaline a product is. It is always tested on a scale of 1 to 14 and if it reads anywhere between 0 to 6.9 it is acidic and if it reads anywhere between 7.1 and 14 it indicates that it is alkaline. A pH of 7 is considered to be neutral. Speaking about your hair, its pH is around 3.7 and that of the scalp is 5.5. As our hair is acidic, it’s important that our shampoo is not alkaline and should be pH balanced between 4.5 and 5.5 for the perfect hair care routine (2).

How does pH Balance in Shampoos Improve Hair Health?

A balanced pH level is extremely important for the health of your hair and scalp and stimulates hair growth. Our hair and scalp are acidic and if you use an alkaline shampoo that has a high pH it creates a negative electrical charge that causes friction between hair fibers causing brittle hair, hair damage and breakage.

Benefits of Using pH-balanced Shampoos

Most shampoos are alkaline but the pH level of our hair is acidic as it's approximately 3.7 and that of our scalp is around 5.5. If you do not use a pH balanced shampoo it will create friction in your hair because of a negative electrical charge causing hair damage and breakage. It will also cause frizziness because the cuticles will swell up and open.Your scalp may also be prone to fungal and bacterial infections. On the other hand, an acidic shampoo will cause the cuticles to shrink and lie flat making the hair softer and smoother. That’s why it’s important to avoid alkaline shampoos and go for one with a pH level between 4.5 and 5.5.

    How to Choose the Best pH-balanced Shampoos?

    Your hair type and texture along with the key  ingredients in the shampoo and what you should avoid, all need to be given due consideration.

    Moisturizing Ingredients

    The main issue with a shampoo that is not pH balanced is that it makes the hair dull and lifeless. To look soft and manageable, your hair should get all the hydration it needs, so look for pH-balanced shampoos that have moisturizing ingredients. Milk proteins and silk proteins as well as aloe vera and argan oil are all moisturizing. As are coconut milk, keratin, and jojoba oil. These ingredients not only hydrate your hair making it smooth and silky but also have a long-lasting effect.

    Ingredients to Avoid

    There are some ingredients commonly used in shampoos that can cause allergic reactions, irritation, and in some extreme cases even damage your scalp. Be careful to avoid these ingredients and try to go for a shampoo that is crafted with natural and skin-friendly ingredients. The things to avoid are harsh ingredients like parabens and phthalates as well as sulfates and alcohol. Synthetic fragrances are also a no-no. These ingredients strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness, itching, and irritation.

    Hair Type

    You need to choose a shampoo that is crafted for your specific hair type. Most shampoos come labeled with this information. 

    • For oily hair, it’s best to avoid a shampoo with a high quantity of lipids or essential oils as it can make your hair heavy and greasy. Our contributor Eliza Penida shares, “The ideal pH level for a good shampoo is between 5.5 to 3.5. For oily hair, you can choose a clarifying shampoo with a pH of up to 5.5, to be used at most only once a week.”
    • For dry hair, you should choose a pH-balanced hydrating shampoo.
    • For thin and fine hair too, avoid a shampoo that has moisturizing ingredients or heavy oils as it makes your hair look limp and greasy.
    • If you have weak hair that breaks easily, a strengthening shampoo with a deep conditioner works wonders in rejuvenating your hair.
    • For hair treated with color go for a gentle shampoo that mentions that it is color-safe and devoid of chemicals.

    Our hair is our crowning glory and it deserves nothing less than the best pH-balanced shampoo to keep it healthy. Sometimes we don’t even realize the damage we are doing to our hair because of the harsh chemicals in it and we need to keep in mind that the perfect pH shampoo must have a gentle formula and be completely chemical-free. It’s important to verify the ingredients thoroughly and ensure that it is safe for your hair. It’s important to consider whether it’s worth the money as an expensive shampoo doesn’t always mean it’s the best. Do remember that the weather, water and food you are exposed to have a large role in deciding which shampoo best suits your hair. If these factors change, you may need to rethink your shampoo as it may no longer work as well.

    What are the benefits of using a pH-balanced shampoo?

    pH-balanced shampoos reduce dryness and frizz in the hair and thus prevent tangling and breakage while also keeping the hair cuticle sealed. pH-balanced shampoos are perfect for all types of hair as they don't strip the scalp or hair of its natural oil.

    What pH level should I look for in a pH-balanced shampoo?

    Whatever type of hair you might have, including colored hair, it’s always recommended to use a shampoo that’s pH balanced between 6 to 7 and should definitely not be higher than that.

    How do pH-balanced shampoos differ from regular shampoos?

    A pH-balanced shampoo means that it has the same acidic pH as your skin and hair which ranges between 4.5 to 5.5. Regular shampoos are more alkaline and have a pH above 7.

    How can I restore the pH balance in my hair?

    If your hair’s pH level has been disrupted, it’s good to try shampoos and oils that can fix it. It’s also best to see a specialist.

    How do I test the pH of my hair?

    The best and easiest way is to use litmus paper to determine the pH level of your hair or the products that you are going to apply to it. The litmus strips should have a range from 0 to 14. Just soak the litmus strip in your shampoo and observe how the color changes.

    Are pH balanced shampoos suitable for all hair types?

    pH balanced shampoos have a pH that ranges between 4.5 and 5.5. They are ideal for deep cleansing your hair without causing any damage to the scalp. Such shampoos are suitable for all hair types whether fine, straight, curly, or thick.

    Can ph imbalance cause hair loss?

    Yes, it can. Human hair and scalp have an acidic pH between 4.5 to 5.5, and when this is disrupted, it would lead to hair breakage and hair fall.

    How often should I use a pH-balanced shampoo?

    A pH-balanced shampoo can be used every day but if your hair is excessively dry it’s best to just use it a few times a week.

    Can pH-balanced shampoos help in maintaining hair color or dyed hair?

    It not only maintains hair color for a longer time, but it also helps to tackle issues like frizziness and tangling.

    Why Use Low pH Shampoos?

    A low pH formula shampoo helps to restore or maintain your hair’s pH at its natural level of 4.5 to 5.5. It prevents moisture loss, removes frizz by reducing static, and keeps the cuticles closed.
