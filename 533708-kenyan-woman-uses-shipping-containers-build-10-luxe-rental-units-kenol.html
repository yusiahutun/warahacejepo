Kenyan Woman Uses Shipping Containers to Build 10 Luxe Rental Units in Kenol

March 19, 2024
blog
  • A Kenyan woman thought out of the box while looking to set up rental units in Murang'a
  • The entrepreneur opted for container units and used wood to give the structure a classy look
  • She built 10 units, which she then listed on Airbnb, and their appearance impressed many netizens

A Kenyan woman has inspired netizens after using an unorthodox building for Airbnb units in Kenol town.

How much do container Airbnb's cost?

Footage shared by Kenyan YouTuber Edna depicted the 10 container homes clad with wood on the exterior, giving a refined look.

The investor fully utilised the limited space by setting up a container shop as the perimeter fence. The entrance leads to the reception area, and another gate leads to the rental units.

Some of the apartments’ selling points include a fully fitted kitchenette, a hot shower, high security, ample parking, free Wi-Fi, a washing machine for laundry and a fireplace.

“It is great for escaping the weekday routine and staying in a serene environment,” said Edna.

The container space between the container units is paved with ballast, and potted plants offer a serene environment. The compound comes alive at night with LED lights and has a fireplace.

The interiors of the houses feature modern, minimalist styling. The kitchenette has two burner gas, enough storage in cabinets, a sink with running water, a microwave and a fridge. Next to the kitchen is the shower with a toilet and sink.

On the other side is a small living space with a sofa set, TV, table and water dispenser. A bed sits at the furthest end of the room.

Guests at the units are charged KSh 2,200 daily.
