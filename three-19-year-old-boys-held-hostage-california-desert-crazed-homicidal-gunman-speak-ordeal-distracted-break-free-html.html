Benjamin Peter Ashley hostages speak out about California desert ordeal

August 31, 2024
blog

Three 19-year-olds who fled for their lives after being taken hostage at gunpoint in the California desert have described their ordeal for the first time.

Keyton Cornelius, Jon Perez and Will Kleier were hiking in the foothills of Kern County on July 28 when Benjamin Peter Ashley ambushed them, lined them up, and threatened to shoot them dead.

He then led them inside a cabin one-by-one, shotgun in hand, and began to line the perimeter with propane tanks.

In a desperate bid for freedom, Will told Ashley their parents were due to arrive at the cabin any minute. He said Ashley could take their car and hide and directed him towards a windy road - giving them time to escape and run 15 miles to the nearest open road.

The new details emerged on Wednesday in an exclusive interview with Good Morning America, days after Ashley was shot dead concluding an epic manhunt through the California wilderness.

Two days after they escaped, 68-year-old dentist David Markiewitz was found dead in a cabin 10 miles away. Authorities believe Ashley shot him.

And on August 1, two SWAT team members were shot and wounded as they searched a mobile home in Kelso Valley. One deputy was shot in both arms, and the other was grazed. They believe it was Ashley. 

Ashley, 34, was shot dead by sheriff's deputies on Saturday following a two-week-long manhunt through the mountainous desert of central California. 

According to the officers, they stopped Ashley as they were walking through the foothills and he pulled out a handgun. He was then shot twice. 

The confrontation came after the owner of a convenience store reported that a man matching Ashley's description had come in and bought about $100 worth of junk food.
