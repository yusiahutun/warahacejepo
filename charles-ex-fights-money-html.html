Charles' ex fights to get her money

June 09, 2024
blog

The lottery of life has not

been kind to Jane Ward,

the one-time girlfriend of

the Prince of Wales.

Nearly three decades after

she caught the eye of Prince

Charles, Jane is virtually penniless and, I

can reveal, is about to resort to the

courts to secure a financial settlement

from ex-husband Andrew Collie.

Jane dated Charles in 1979 when she

was a vivacious 25-year-old and he

reportedly seduced her in the bushes at

a polo party. The pair are also said to

have enjoyed romps in the privacy of

her secluded cottage in Windsor.

Now life is more humdrum for

53-year-old Jane. After her fling with

the Prince, her first marriage failed

before she wed farmer-turned-telecommunications

surveyor Collie

and settled down to become a housewife

and mother of their three children.

But despite divorcing her husband 12

years ago she is — bizarrely — only now

pursuing him for a settlement.

Scroll down for more...

"I can't make ends meet any longer,"

Jane has been telling friends. Her

application for a financial settlement

will be heard this week at Nottingham

County Court.

Jane's marriage fell apart in 1995.

Divorce followed, but the couple

surprised friends by getting back

together three years later, although

they never remarried.

Sadly, their unwedded bliss did not

last and, as I revealed, the relationship

became strained again last year.

Collie, 50, fell for another woman —

whom Jane describes as 'an Amazonian

blonde barrister' — and recently moved

out of the family's six-bedroom farm house in Leicestershire.

Now Jane claims he is no longer on speaking terms

with their children — Felicia, 20, and her

brothers Tom, 23, and Marcus, 22, who

are semi-professional polo players.

She also says she has had to take in

lodgers to pay bills and claims Collie

intends to sell the house, plus the

cottage next door.

This would mean Jane's widowed

stepmother, 80-year-old Diane Herring,

would lose her home of ten years and

that Jane would have to relocate her

livery business.

"I have been having an awful time,"

Jane tells me. "I feel I've got no other

choice but to apply to the court for a

financial settlement."

Last night, Collie was unavailable for

comment.

Claire won't raise a glass to the English

Her boyfriend is English

and she has just made

her Broadway debut as

cockney Eliza Doolittle

in Pygmalion, so it's not

surprising to find sultry

American actress Claire

Danes is something of

an Anglophile.

Claire, 28, who has been

dating dreamy Oxfordeducated

actor Hugh Dancy for a year, admits

she has fallen in love

with British comedy.

"You guys are funnier

and more ironic," says

Claire, who also puts on

an English accent for

her role in Matthew

Vaughn's fantasy film

Stardust. "I'm obsessed

with Catherine Tate and

anything Steve Coogan-related."

But one British trait has

failed to charm Claire —

binge drinking.

"I'm learning about pub

culture," says Claire. "Americans drink,

but we don't station

ourselves somewhere

for six hours and drink.

That's tedious and I

can't do it."

Tzuke's new generation

More mature aficionados of chart

music will remember singer Judie

Tzuke's raunchy single Stay With Me

Till Dawn.

Now almost three

decades after her 1979 hit, Judie is

helping her 20-year-old daughter

Bailey to embark on a career in

pop, with the launch of her debut

single Uninvited.

But Bailey, who has sung

backing vocals on all her mother's

tours since deciding she wanted to

be a singer at the age of 14, admits

her song is strait-laced compared

with her mother's breakthrough hit

— described by Judy as "a little dirty".

"It's a dance track cover of an

Alanis Morissette song and is not

romantic at all. My mother's song

was very sexy," says Bailey, who

shares the family mansion in Surrey

with her mother, music producer

father Paul and musician boyfriend

Matthew Roacher.

Adds Judie: "My song was about an

emotional exchange with a friend I

had one night, but everybody

thought it was a bit too sexy."

At the age of 47, Hugh Grant may

be no closer to becoming a

husband, let alone a father.

But I hear he has just taken on the role of

godfather to Edith and Isaac, the

seven-month-old twins of his close

pals Nicola Formby, 41, Tatler

magazine's editor at large, and her

partner, Adrian Gill, better known as

the food critic A.A. Gill.

Other

godparents include Jeremy Clarkson

and designer Lucy Sisman. Says a

friend: "It was a lovely christening at a

church, but Nicola looked tired."

At least Gill has experience of looking

after babies. The 53-year-old scribe

has two older children — Flora, 16, and

Alasdair, 14 — with his second wife

Amber Rudd.

Spare a thought for author

Mark McCrum — he is about to

break the family tradition of

sending McCrum boys to Eton

because it is too expensive.

Mark and his wife Jo Hodder — the

assistant general secretary of the

Society of Authors who was once

described as the country's prettiest

trade unionist — are expecting their

first child in December.

But Mark, whose late father Michael was Head

Master of Eton for ten years from

1970, tells me he is unlikely to see a

son follow in his footsteps.

"It's a great place and it did me a lot

of good, but I just couldn't afford it,"'

says Mark, who clearly did not

make a fortune from writing the

authorised biography of Robbie

Williams, Somebody Someday. "The

fees are now nearly £25,000 a year."

Mark and Jo married in Cambridge

six months ago and she became

pregnant on their honeymoon in

St Lucia. But he clearly hasn't lost

all hope of signing that elusive

big-money book deal.

"I suppose it would do no harm to

put his name down — just in case."

Oscar Humphries has never made any secret

of his preference for seeing his father Barry

performing as a man rather than Dame Edna Everage.

But Oscar, 26, who has grown closer to his father since

conquering a drug addiction, was the first person

Barry turned to for approval after appearing as his

cross-dressing alter ego at the Fashion Rocks event at

the Royal Albert Hall.

"How was I?" asked the out-of-costume Barry as he

took a seat next to Oscar to watch the rest of the show.

"Dad, you were great," replied the loyal Oscar. "Just

great and really funny." Ah, bless!
