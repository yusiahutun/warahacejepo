Al Sharpton is an outspoken preacher and civil rights activist known for leading the fight against racial prejudice and injustice in the United States of America. Some describe him as a controversial, racist arsonist, and his critics say he is a political activist who should be held responsible for the deterioration of racial relations.

To the black American population, however, he is a hero who is willing to go to prison for promoting their cause. Former US President Barrack Obama described him as “the voice of the voiceless and a champion of the oppressed”.

Al Sharpton – Bio/Wiki

Al Sharpton was born on 3 October 1954 as Alfred Charles Sharpton Jr. in Brooklyn New York City. Sharpton attended public schools in Queens and Brooklyn. He graduated from Samuel J. Tilden High School in 1972 and was enrolled at Brooklyn College, where he was a major in contemporary politics before dropping out after two years. During his school years, he became involved in civil rights movements, held several leadership positions, and participated in several high-profile protests aimed at achieving equality between black and white Americans.

While dropping out of school, he founded his own organization, which was named the National Youth Movement. The organization’s activities helped to keep the media’s attention on the racially motivated murder of a black teenager named Michael Griffith in 1986.

As a child and a churchgoer ordained as a pastor at the age of 10, Sharpton developed his confident style of speech. He was best described as a socio-political activist and religious leader who became a leading fighter and a roaring voice in the fight against racial prejudice and injustice against Americans of black origin. In this struggle, he got into various problems and even survived an assassination attempt. But whatever problems he had, he remained true to his activism by organizing more protests and giving press conferences whenever and for whatever purpose they took place.

He fought at various times against being elected to several political offices but is not known to have been an elected official. On one of his unsuccessful political trips, he hinted that winning political office may not have been his ultimate goal.
