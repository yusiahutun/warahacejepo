Introduction

Bucky Dent is an American former Major League Baseball player who was voted the World Series Most Valuable Player in 1978.

He currently hosts a podcast, Deep to Left with Bucky Dent.

Biography

He was born on November 25, 1951, in Savannah, Georgia, the USA, as Russell Earl O’Dey.

Dent graduated from Hialeah High School.

In 1970, he was the sixth overall pick in the MLB draft.

In 1973, Dent started to play for the Chicago White Sox.

In 1975, Bucky earned his first All-Star selection.

In early April 1977, he was traded to the New York Yankees.

Home Run

At the end of the 1978 regular season, Dent hit a three-run home run in a tie-breaker game against the Boston Red Sox. The game was played at Fenway Park in Boston.

”I didn’t realize it when that ball went out of here, but that home run changed my life,” Bucky said in an interview in 2016. He added: ”It changed my life because 38 years later, people are still talking about it. It’s one of the greatest baseball games ever. It’s history.”

He finished his career while playing for Kansas City Royals in 1984.

From 1989 to 1990, Dent served as a manager for the New York Yankees.

Wife

On November 1, 1985, Bucky Dent married Marianne Seywent.

”I met Marianne on a flight during the 1981 season,” Bucky told Yankees. He also said: ”I asked her for her phone number, but she told me that she didn’t like giving it out. Eventually, I got her to give me her number.”

Marianne passed away on October 22, 2015. She had been diagnosed with brain cancer 19 months prior to her death.

In November 2019, Dent married Angie Wildstein.
