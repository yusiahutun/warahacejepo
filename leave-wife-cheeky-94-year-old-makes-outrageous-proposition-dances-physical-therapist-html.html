Georgia pensioner makes outrageous proposition as she dances with her physical therapist

June 06, 2024
blog

This is the hilarious moment a mischievous 94-year-old tells her carer to leave his wife as they dance together.

While Pearl Moses and her carer Larry Ozoh slowly swayed in her home in Lawrenceville, Georgia, on December 8 - their last physical therapy appointment - the pair discussed her relationship status, along with daughter Janet Hanson.

Off camera, Janet mentions that if her nonagenarian mother had somebody to take her dancing she would be a lot stronger, leading Larry to comment 'we got to find her a boyfriend'.

With a cheeky glint in her eye and naughty grin on her face, Pearl says Larry 'should leave his wife', leaving everyone in the room in stitches.

Janet said: 'I thought it was hilarious and precious all at the same time.

'But I was a little taken aback when mom said that.

'Larry has been amazing as he has had to persevere with her, even when she was difficult.

'He asked her to dance once before and it made her day as mum loves music.

'This time it was a little bittersweet since it was Larry's last appointment with mom.'

Larry has visited Pearl twice a week since the end of October 2016 after she fell down basement stairs in September.

She suffered a large hematoma on her head, broke her left shoulder, one rib, punctured her lung, fractured her hip and was in hospital for six days before returning to Janet's home.

Initially, Pearl wasn't receptive to Larry, but he remained determined to help her, eventually leading to this touching moment.

Janet said: 'At first mom was resistant to him and considered his visits an intrusion, partly because of pain, partly because she wanted to be left alone to sleep.

'She was even hostile at times to any therapy.

'But his manner was less about doing his job and more about seeing what he could do to reach her.

'He asked what she enjoyed - music, dancing, playing bridge, playing Texas hold 'em.

'I heard him use endearing terms when he talked to her and she began to respond to his tenderness.

'We have grown to love Larry and we have vowed to maintain the friendship.'

Her daughter says as her mum has grown older, she has become even more of a flirt, proving this comment wasn't especially out of character.

Janet said: 'She loves men and would love to have a boyfriend.

'But there are not many out there her age.

'Prior to the fall, Mom and I played poker every Wednesday.

'The games are attended mostly by younger men, but she flirted with them all, young and old.

'They responded in kind, which is very special, and everyone loves her bawdy sense of humour and her spunk.

'They cannot believe it when they get beaten by a 94-year-old woman.

'In fact, they can't believe she's 94.'
