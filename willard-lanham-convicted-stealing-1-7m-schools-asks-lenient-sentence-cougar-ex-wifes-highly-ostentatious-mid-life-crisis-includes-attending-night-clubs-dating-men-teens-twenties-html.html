A technology consultant convicted of stealing $1.7 million from the New York City Department of Education is asking the court for leniency - claiming his ex-wife's sexual conquests and midlife crisis means no one will be able to look after their daughter.

Willard 'Ross' Lanham, 58, was found guilty in March of four counts of theft and fraud after it was discovered he had stolen money which was designated to provide students with internet access.

He faces up to 70 years in jail when convicted, but claimed recently in court papers that his ex-wife, self-described cougar Laura Lanham is going through a 'highly ostentatious mid-life crisis' which includes 'attending night clubs and dating men in their teens and twenties'.

Request: Willard 'Ross' Lanham is asking the court for a lenient sentence so he can provide financial and emotional support to their teenage daughter

He said the situation has left him unable to provide financial and emotional support and guidance to their 16-year-old daughter, according to the New York Post.

Defense lawyer Stephen Prezioso wrote in a court filing: 'A low sentence in this case would allow Mr Lanham to maintain the close contacts he has with his children and especially with his youngest daughter.'

Manhattan prosecutors want to send him away for nine years for the theft and fraud.

Speaking to the New York Post yesterday about her husband's allegations, Ms Lanham unleashed an expletive-filled tirade against ther ex-husband, calling his allegations 'such a f***ing lie'.

'I go out with my 40-year-old friends because I’m single and I’m allowed to go out,' she told the paper. 'I don’t sleep around like he likes to make everybody think.

'He's a master manipulator who has been a cheater forever. If he thought I was a bitch before, I’m going to show him a bitch.'

The couple have been living in the same Long Island home they shared when they were together because of financial issues. 

When her husband was first arrested for the theft, the 42-year-old said she was going to become America's next big reality star.

Prior to this, she was a little-known internet blogger on a site dedicated to life as a cougar. When her Lanham hit the headlines, Cougarlife quickly became an internet sensation.

The brunette mother-of-three told a U.S. newspaper at the time: ‘My life is already a reality show.

‘You can’t make this s**t up. My reality is surreal, it’s crazy.’

Ms Lanham separated from her husband four years ago, but due to financial problems they still live in the same home on Laura Lane – a street built and named after her.

‘It’s a big house,’ she explained. ‘We have separate rooms, I’m happy. I love my life.’

Following the split, Mrs Lanham said she set up the blog after posing for nude photos that she send to Playboy.

She said she embraced life as a cougar after the break up and enjoys the attention she gets from younger men.

She said: ‘For me, I love it. It’s flirty, empowering and I meet some of the most incredible people.

‘Older men I find usually want a significant partner. I turn that off quick, I am not looking for a long-term commitment. I am looking for fun and excitement with no ties.

‘Remember, you are as young and beautiful as you feel. I feel like I am 30.

But she acknowledged that the newly-found attention has created problems for her 16-year-old daughter and two step-children, who are 21 and 24 years old.

‘They saw the comments people made about their dad and I, and it hurts,’ she said.

‘No one wants to see their mother called a whore.’
