Biography

Neil Simon was born in The Bronx, NY on July 4, 1927. New York playwright who wrote such popular plays as Barefoot in the Park, The Odd Couple, and Biloxi Blues. Across his career, he received more combined Tony and Oscar Award nominations than any other writer. In 1950, he wrote scripts for comedian Sid Caesar‘s variety show Your Show of Shows. He grew up during The Great Depression and graduated from DeWitt Clinton High School at age sixteen, where he earned the nickname Doc.
