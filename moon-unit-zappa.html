Moon Unit Zappa is an actress, singer, author, and celebrity kid from the United States. Moon Unit Zappa is widely known as the eldest daughter of musician Frank Zappa. She provided vocals for her father’s song ‘Valley Girl’ in 1982.

Early Life

On the 28th of September 1967, Moon Unit Zappa was born in New York City, the United States of America. She is 55 years old and has a birth sign of Libra. In addition, she belongs to a mixed ethnicity and practices the Christian religion.

She was born to her father Frank Zappa and his second wife Gail Zappa. By profession, her father was a musician, composer, and bandleader. In contrast, her mother was a musician and composer. Zappa’s father had Sicilian, Greek-Arab, and French ancestors, while her mother had German and Portuguese ancestors. She grew up with her three younger siblings: Dweezil, Ahmet, and Diva.

Moving on to her academics, she attended the independent day school Oakwood School in the Fernando Valley region of Los Angeles. It is unclear whether she has completed her graduation or not.

Professional Career

Moon Unit Zappa first appeared on screen when her father, Frank Zappa, decided to cast her in one of his music videos. She appeared in the song ‘Valley Girl’ from her father’s 1982 album “Ship Arriving Too Late to Save a Drowning Witch.” As a result, she became a public figure. The music video was a huge success, reaching number 32 on the Billboard Hot 100 in the United States.

Zappa appeared in her brother’s debut single, ‘My Mother Is a Space Cadet,’ which she also co-wrote. Soon after, the siblings gained popularity, which led to television offers, and they appeared on MTV several times throughout the 1980s.

She made her television debut in the 1982 crime drama series “CHiPs” playing Tami. The next year, she made her film debut in the horror anthology film “Nightmares” playing the role of Pamela. This movie was directed by, Joseph Sargent.

In the following years, she appeared as a guest actress on TV shows such as “The Facts of Life” (1985), “Student Exchange” (1987), “Curb Your Enthusiasm”(2004), “How I Met Your Mother” (2006), “Grey’s Anatomy” (2010) and “High School USA!” (2013).

Zappa appeared in six episodes of the TV series “Fast Times” as Barbara in 1986, and 13 episodes of the comedy series “Normal Life” as Tess Harlow in 1990. Moreover, she also appeared in supporting roles in films such as “Little Sister”(1992), “Dark Side of Genius” (1994), “Love Always” (1996), “The Girl Gets Moe”(1997), “Behind the Seams” (2000), and “Last Will” (2011).
