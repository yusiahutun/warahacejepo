Mom, 64, stabbed to death in San Francisco apartment building - niece slams city's 'severe problems'

February 15, 2024
blog

An elderly mother was found stabbed to death inside her San Francisco apartment building after being slain by her next-door neighbor. 

Mei Ran Hu, 64, was pronounced dead at the scene after being found with severe stab wounds on May 5, leading to the arrest of her neighbor Jesus Esparza, 41, hours later. 

The incident occurred while Hu stepped out to retrieve her mail, and the two neighbors had never spoken before, according to KGO-TV reporter Dion Lim. 

Neighbors have described the suspect as having serious mental health issues, and Hu's niece slammed the senseless killing, saying: 'I want her death to be a reminder we have severe problems in our society in San Francisco.' 

It comes as San Francisco has been captivated by a rampant crime wave fueled by untamed homelessness and soft-on-crime governing, punctuated by the recent slaying of CashApp founder Bob Lee last month.

Mei Ran Hu, 64, was stabbed to death May 5 inside her San Francisco apartment building

The San Francisco Police Department said they identified Esparza as a suspect and quickly detained him within the same building Hu was stabbed to death inside. 

Esparza allegedly knifed Hu three times in the lobby of the building, before she attempted to flee and hid in a nearby stairwell. 

The victim's family said they believe her attempts to hide meant others inside the building failed to find her, and she lay in hiding for roughly an hour, Lim reported on social media. 

Hu was eventually discovered unresponsive at approximately 11am, and she succumbed to her injuries soon after first responders arrived on the scene. 

Esparza was booked into San Francisco County Jail on charges of murder, elder abuse, and resisting arrest. 

No motive has yet been released for the slaying, however staff and neighbors say Esparza suffered serious mental health issues and relied on the care of his mother, according to MissionLocal. 

His mother was reportedly not in the building at the time of the stabbing, and an individual familiar with the situation said: 'If the mom was here, I don't think this would happen'. 

The victim's son, pictured, said he was concerned his mother would 'die for nothing' if the suspect is released 

Hu had been caring for her elderly mother for almost 10 years before her death months ago

The horror stabbing unfolded on the 400 block of Duboce Avenue 

Hu's son, speaking to KGO-TV, said he was concerned the suspect in the case would escape justice. 

'Probably, he can be released after, and (my) mom will die for nothing,' he said. 

Hu's family reportedly said Esparza lived in the unit next to Hu's in Duboce Triangle, San Francisco, however the two never met due to their language barrier. 

The 64-year-old had reportedly moved into the apartment to serve as her mother's caretaker, which she had done for almost 10 years before her mother passed several months ago. 

Neighbors inside the apartment block told Mission Local that Esparza was arrested by police in his 'drawers' on the ground floor hallway. 

'I've seen him a lot,' said one resident, who described the 41-year-old as a 'violent person'. 

'I know he's got mental health issues. Like, serious,' they added. 

The neighbor also recalled an incident several months ago in which a tenant's dog approached the Esparza with its 'tail wagging, happy', before the suspected killer 'kicked the dog violently'. 

San Francisco has been blighted by a string of retail closures since the pandemic as the city has failed to deal with rising crime and drug problems

San Francisco has in recent months seen a worrying uptick in murders and robberies  

The slaying comes as a crime wave continues to tear through San Francisco, leading the city to become a ghost town as huge numbers of residents flee the area. 

New data found that the West Coast city is just 32 percent as full as it was pre-pandemic, an issue that has largely been blamed on lawlessness and soft-on-crime policies. 

The crime wave was punctuated by the killing of tech executive Bob Lee last month, however it came as residents have endured years of untamed violence, open-air drug markets and rampant homelessness. 

Alongside the prevalence of drugs such as fentanyl, the city has also witnessed a sharp uptick in homicides and robberies, while numerous major retailers including T-Mobile have closed their San Francisco locations due to 'rampant criminal activity'.
