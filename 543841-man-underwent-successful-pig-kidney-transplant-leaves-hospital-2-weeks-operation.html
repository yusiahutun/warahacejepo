Man Who Underwent Successful Pig Kidney Transplant Leaves Hospital 2 Weeks after Operation

March 16, 2024
blog
  • Richard "Rick" Slayman, 62, has made history as the first human being to receive a pig kidney transplant
  • The genetically modified kidney transplant was done by medics at Massachusetts General Hospital
  • Slayman left the hospital on Wednesday, April 3, two weeks after undergoing the historic successful kidney transplant in medical history

Michael Ollinga is a journalist at TUKO.co.ke with over 10 years of experience covering courts and crimes, special reports, and current affairs in Kenya.

Rick Slayman, 62, silently made history after he was discharged from the hospital after undergoing a successful pig kidney transplant.

Man undergoes successful pig kidney transplant

According to medics at Massachusetts General Hospital, the pig's kidney transplanted to Slayman was genetically modified.

According to the hospital, Slayman was battling an end-stage kidney disease and was undergoing dialysis until the life-changing four-hour operation conducted on Saturday, March 16.

This makes Slayman the world's first living recipient of a pig kidney that was genetically edited to work in a human body.

“We are happy to share that, Rick Slayman, our first patient to ever receive a genetically edited pig kidney transplant, has been discharged from the hospital. He is recovering well and will continue to recuperate at home with his family,” the hospital posted on its X account on Thursday, April 4.

Richard "Rick" Slayman speaks after kidney transplant

"This moment of leaving the hospital today with one of the cleanest bills of health I have had in a long time is one that I wished would come for many years. Now, it's a reality and on of the happiest moments of my life," Slayman said in a statement posted by the hospital.

Slayman is a manager with the Massachusetts Department of Transportation.

He disclosed that the medics had told him to try a pig kidney when he was diagnosed with an end-stage kidney disease in 2023.

After accepting the suggestion, the medics said the pig kidney could last many years.

Animal-to-human transplants

The medics also acknowledged that many unknowns existed about animal-to-human transplants.

Slayman thanked the Massachusetts General Hospital fraternity, which cared for him before and after the historic transplant.

He mentioned doctors, Williams, Riella, Kawai, and numerous nurses who looked after him every day he was at the facility.

A new beginning for kidney disease patients

"The care I received was exceptional,, and I trust MGH with my life. I’m excited to resume spending time with my family, friends, and loved ones, free from the burden of dialysis that has affected my quality of life for many years. "Lastly, I want to thank anyone who has seen my story and sent well wishes, especially patients waiting for a kidney transplant. Today marks a new beginning, not just for me but for them, as well. My recovery is progressing smoothly, and I ask for privacy now," Slayman said.
