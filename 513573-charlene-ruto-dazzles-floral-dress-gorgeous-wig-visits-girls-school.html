President William Ruto's daughter Charlene Ruto has stepped out in a lovely floral dress and new wig during a visit to a girls' high school in Baringo.

The president's daughter visited the school for a church service and to educate the learners on Climate Change and the conservation of the environment.

In pictures shared by climate activist Anita Soina who had accompanied the first daughter, Charlene, as usual, she brought her A-game in fashion as she stunned in a stylish black floral dress that was nearly floral length.

To complete the look, she used Maasai accessories on her neck and wig.

"I accompanied Our Youth and Climate Champion, Charlene Ruto, to Lake Bogoria Girls’ Secondary school in Baringo County for a Church Service," Anita said.

According to Anita, they both held Climate Education Forum where they discussed the role of young people and the community in general in contributing to Climate Action.

Charlene Ruto's love for Climate Action

The first daughter has been vocal on matters of climate change and action and has been planting trees during her country-wide visits.

TUKO.co.ke reported when she visited Isiolo county and engaged the region's leaders on climate change.

Charlene, who was elated to visit the county, met Isiolo deputy governor James Lowasa and his team members at the Isiolo county government offices.
